The Problem

Autonomous refuelling robot arms need to reliably detect the vehicle's fuel flap — even when the nozzle or arm partially obstructs the view. Standard CNN-based detectors rely on local feature aggregation. When the defining features of the fuel flap (its circular rim, hinge) are occluded, the CNN simply has no local pixel evidence to trigger a detection.

The core question: can attention-based architectures see around occlusion by using global scene context, rather than relying on the locally visible features of the target itself?

Dataset

A synthetic occluded dataset of 3,783 images generated using OpenCV and Albumentations. Occlusions were applied programmatically to simulate the robotic arm and nozzle blocking the fuel flap at varying positions and severities. Evaluation used a unified confidence threshold of 0.50 for direct comparison across all three architectures.

Why CNNs Fail Under Occlusion

YOLOv11 exhibits 1,759 false negatives — it misses 46.5% of the dataset. When the circular rim or hinge of the fuel flap is obscured, the CNN has no local pixel data to trigger a detection. RF-DETR's self-attention processes global context — the vehicle's fender and body panels — allowing it to infer the flap's presence even when the flap is hidden. False negatives drop to 417 (11%).

Confusion counts at IoU=0.50. OCSA reduces false negatives from 1,759 → 193. A 5.1% miss rate.

YOLOv11-OCSA: Best of Both Worlds

The OCSA (Occlusion-aware Channel and Spatial Attention) modules are integrated into YOLOv11's backbone and neck. They add channel and spatial attention that lets the network focus on non-occluded regions and leverage global context — achieving Transformer-level robustness while retaining CNN inference speed. Overhead: ~2.3M additional parameters.

Speed-accuracy tradeoff. YOLOv11-OCSA occupies the ideal top-right quadrant: highest F1 at fastest speed.

Conclusion

YOLOv11's 46.5% false negative rate is a critical failure mode for robotic control — nearly half of refuelling attempts would not initiate. RF-DETR provides reliable detection but only at 14 FPS. YOLOv11-OCSA hits 5.1% miss rate (below the 10% safety threshold) at 105 FPS, a 7.5× speedup over RF-DETR with superior accuracy. It is the recommended architecture for deployment.

Broader finding: attention mechanisms — whether full Transformers or lightweight attention modules bolted onto CNNs — are fundamentally more robust to occlusion than pure local feature aggregation.
